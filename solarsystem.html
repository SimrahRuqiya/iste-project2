Our solar system is an amazingly complex ensemble of the celestially bound objects circling around the Sun, a medium-sized star. These constitute eight planets that are quite different in structure, size, and distances from the Sun. Opposite to the inner rocky planets are the outer gas giants: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. All these put up a dynamic system surrounded by moons, asteroids, and comets, somewhat endlessly mysterious.
