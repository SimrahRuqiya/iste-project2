Student-Built Capsules Endure Heat of Re-entry

In July 2024, five student-built capsules withstood scorching heat while reentering Earth's atmosphere in a second Kentucky ReEntry Probe Experiment called KREPE2. The researchers are still processing the data derived from the KREPE2 experiments, but their findings could sharpen the features of heat shields that would protect the space crafts during their return to Earth.

Transform In-Space Manufacturing with Laser Beam Welding Collaboration

As plans take shape for sending humans back to the Moon, and eventually on to Mars, this laser beam welding collaboration between NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Ala. and The Ohio State University in Columbus is poised to spur along in-space manufacturing.

November 2024 Skywatching Tips
